If you are looking for the Climbro training plan in one page, the important thing to understand is this:

Climbro does **not** publish a fixed universal 8-week calendar on its public site.

What it does publish is a step-by-step workflow:

1. complete the Performance Test Battery
2. identify your weak point
3. generate a personal training plan
4. follow the sessions in the app
5. retest later

That is the real Climbro training process.

## Quick Answer

Climbro's public training workflow starts with testing, not guesswork.

The idea is simple:

- use the Performance Test Battery to see what needs work
- get a training plan aimed at the weakest point
- follow the guided sessions in the app
- retest later and compare progress

So if you are searching for a "Climbro training plan," the best way to think about it is not as a fixed eight-week template.

It is a test-to-plan system that adapts around your current results.

## Why Climbers Care About This Workflow

This matters because many climbers do not really need another generic hangboard schedule.

They need:

- a clearer idea of what to train first
- less guesswork about whether they are improving
- a repeatable way to test, train, and retest
- a system that feels more structured than random home sessions

That is the appeal of Climbro's workflow.

It is not just "here are some hangs."

It is "here is the testing layer, here is the weak point, here is the next plan."

## Step 1: Complete the Performance Test Battery

On Climbro's <ClimbroTrackedLink href="https://climbro.com/how-it-works/" label="How it works" intent="how_it_works" source="climbro-training-plan" position="body_inline" /> page, the first step is the **Performance Test Battery**.

That page describes a four-test battery that feeds into the broader training system.

Climbro's public pages are not perfectly consistent about every named test across the site, but the main role of the battery is clear:

- test key parts of finger and forearm performance
- identify strong points and weak points
- use the results to guide the next training phase

The same public workflow says the results are presented as climbing grades in a radar chart so users can identify strengths and weaknesses.

## Step 2: Do the Battery Under the Right Conditions

Climbro's official instructions say:

- perform the tests when fully recovered
- read the test descriptions carefully in the app
- rest sufficiently between tests
- complete the battery over several days if needed
- finish all four tests within a maximum of two weeks

The same page says the battery results stay valid for one month.

## Step 3: Generate a Personal Training Plan

After the battery, Climbro says users can get a personal training plan to improve their weakest point.

According to the official site, the plan:

- consists of 16 to 24 training sessions
- includes several exercises per session
- uses different intensity, repetitions, work times, and rest times
- is tracked inside the app

Climbro also says the app guides users on when to train or rest and evaluates how well the schedule is being followed.

## Step 4: Retest and Compare Progress

Climbro's official process does not end with one plan.

The <ClimbroTrackedLink href="https://climbro.com/how-it-works/" label="How it works" intent="how_it_works" source="climbro-training-plan" position="body_inline" /> page says that after completing the current training plan, users can:

- do the Performance Test Battery again
- compare current and previous results
- get a new training plan

That retest-and-repeat loop is the main official training flow published on Climbro's website.

That is also why this page should not be read like a fixed calendar article.

The process is cyclical:

- test
- train
- retest
- adjust

## What Climbro Says About Single Tests and Exercises

Climbro's `How it works` page also says the tests from the Performance Test Battery can be performed individually.

The page recommends performing the **Max Strength** test regularly because its results are used to determine the absolute intensity of other tests and training exercises.

Climbro also describes additional app functions tied to specific capacities, including:

- Finger RFD for explosive finger strength
- Continuous for aerobic endurance
- Intermittent for power-endurance
- All Out for anaerobic endurance

That matters because it shows Climbro is not only publishing one battery and one plan.

It is also publishing a broader measurement-and-exercise framework around specific capacities.

## What the Official Trial Page Recommends

On the 30-Day Home Trial page, Climbro says the first 30 days can be used to:

- complete the Performance Test Battery
- assess current climbing form
- identify areas to improve
- create a tailored training plan

That is the closest thing the official site publishes to a starter timeline.

It also helps explain why the Climbro workflow appeals to buyers who want structure before they commit long term.

## App Access: Free vs Premium

Climbro's FAQ says:

- the app is free for core hangboard features
- free access includes Max-Strength Tests, Single Exercises, Logs, Analytics, and more
- the Performance Test Battery and Personal Training Plan are premium features

If you want the fully guided test-to-plan workflow, this is the key distinction.

The free layer gives access to core hangboard features.

The premium layer is where Climbro puts the full battery and personal-plan logic.

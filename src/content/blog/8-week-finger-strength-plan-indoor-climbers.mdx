> **Disclosure:** This page was rewritten to match Climbro's official website in March 2026.

If you are looking for Climbro-specific training information, the official site does **not** publish a fixed 8-week schedule. What it does publish is a step-by-step process built around testing, app guidance, and a personal training plan.

This page now follows that official process only.

## Step 1: Complete the Performance Test Battery

On Climbro's `How it works` page, the first step is the **Performance Test Battery**. Climbro says it consists of four tests:

- Max Strength
- Finger RFD
- Continuous
- Intermittent

Climbro says these tests measure:

- maximal finger strength
- rate of force development
- forearm aerobic capacity
- forearm anaerobic capacity

The official page says the results are presented as climbing grades in a radar chart so users can identify strengths and weaknesses.

## Step 2: Do the Battery Under the Right Conditions

Climbro's official instructions say:

- perform the tests when fully recovered
- read the test descriptions carefully in the app
- rest sufficiently between tests
- complete the battery over several days if needed
- finish all four tests within a maximum of two weeks

The same page says the battery results stay valid for one month.

## Step 3: Generate a Personal Training Plan

After the battery, Climbro says users can get a personal training plan to improve their weakest point.

According to the official site, the plan:

- consists of 16 to 24 training sessions
- includes several exercises per session
- uses different intensity, repetitions, work times, and rest times
- is tracked inside the app

Climbro also says the app guides users on when to train or rest and evaluates how well the schedule is being followed.

## Step 4: Retest and Compare Progress

Climbro's official process does not end with one plan.

The `How it works` page says that after completing the current training plan, users can:

- do the Performance Test Battery again
- compare current and previous results
- get a new training plan

That retest-and-repeat loop is the main official training flow published on Climbro's website.

## What Climbro Says About Single Tests and Exercises

Climbro's `How it works` page also says the tests from the Performance Test Battery can be performed individually.

The page recommends performing the **Max Strength** test regularly because its results are used to determine the absolute intensity of other tests and training exercises.

Climbro also describes additional app functions tied to specific capacities, including:

- Finger RFD for explosive finger strength
- Continuous for aerobic endurance
- Intermittent for power-endurance
- All Out for anaerobic endurance

## What the Official Trial Page Recommends

On the 30-Day Home Trial page, Climbro says the first 30 days can be used to:

- complete the Performance Test Battery
- assess current climbing form
- identify areas to improve
- create a tailored training plan

That is the closest thing the official site publishes to a "starter plan" timeline.

## App Access: Free vs Premium

Climbro's FAQ says:

- the app is free for core hangboard features
- free access includes Max-Strength Tests, Single Exercises, Logs, Analytics, and more
- the Performance Test Battery and Personal Training Plan are premium features

## Final Take

If you want the official Climbro process in one page, it is this:

1. complete the four-test Performance Test Battery
2. generate a personal training plan
3. follow the 16 to 24 session plan in the app
4. retest and compare results

If your fingers fail before your technique does, this plan is for you.

You do not need a complicated protocol. You need consistency, recovery, and progressive load.

## Quick Answer

Train finger strength twice per week for 8 weeks, keep climbing sessions skill-focused, and track one performance metric weekly. Most climbers improve if they avoid maxing out too often.

## Weekly Structure

- Day 1: Finger strength session (high quality, low volume)
- Day 2: Technique climbing (easy to moderate)
- Day 3: Rest
- Day 4: Finger strength session (moderate intensity)
- Day 5: Climbing session (projecting)
- Day 6: Active recovery or mobility
- Day 7: Rest

## Session Template (25-35 min)

### Warm-up (10 min)

- 3-5 easy boulders or easy route laps
- shoulder + wrist prep
- 2 progressive hangs at low intensity

### Main work (12-18 min)

Choose one edge depth you can control with good form.

- 5 sets
- 10-second hang
- 2-3 minutes rest between sets

For week 1-2, stay submaximal. You should finish with one rep in reserve.

### Optional finish (5 min)

- light forearm extensor work
- scapular pulls

## 8-Week Progression

### Weeks 1-2: Base

Goal: perfect form and repeatability.

### Weeks 3-4: Load

Add small intensity progression (load, reduced assistance, or smaller edge).

### Weeks 5-6: Consolidate

Keep volume stable. Do not chase ego jumps.

### Weeks 7-8: Performance block

Push one session slightly harder, keep second session controlled.

## Common Mistakes

1. Going max too early
2. Adding too much volume while still climbing hard
3. Not resting enough between hangs
4. Ignoring skin and connective tissue fatigue

## Tracking That Actually Helps

Track one metric each week:

- max controlled 10-second hang on your test edge
- or total quality sets completed at a fixed load

If your quality drops for 2 consecutive sessions, deload for 4-7 days.

## FAQ

### Should beginners do finger training?

If you're brand new, prioritize technique and movement first. Add dedicated finger sessions once you have basic climbing volume tolerance.

### How sore is too sore?

Mild fatigue is fine. Sharp finger pain or tendon pain is a stop signal.

### Can I climb hard and finger train hard in same week?

You can, but most indoor climbers progress faster with one hard finger day and one moderate day.

## Final Take

The best plan is the one you can execute for 8 straight weeks.

Start conservative, progress slowly, and protect recovery. That is how finger strength actually compounds.

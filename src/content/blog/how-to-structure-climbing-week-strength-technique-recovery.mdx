Most climbers do too much hard climbing back-to-back, then wonder why progress stalls.

A better week has clear intent per session.

## Quick Answer

Use a 3-session template: one technique day, one strength/power day, one projecting day, with at least 2 real recovery days.

## The 3-Session Framework

### Session 1: Technique + Volume

- easy to moderate grades
- focus on footwork, pacing, and route reading
- leave with energy in the tank

### Session 2: Strength/Power

- shorter session
- high-quality attempts
- long rests between hard efforts

### Session 3: Projecting

- work specific climbs near your limit
- film attempts if possible
- stop when quality drops

## Suggested Weekly Calendar

- Monday: Technique + Volume
- Tuesday: Rest
- Wednesday: Strength/Power
- Thursday: Active recovery
- Friday: Projecting
- Saturday: Optional easy movement
- Sunday: Full rest

## Recovery Rules That Matter

1. Sleep 7-9 hours
2. Keep one full rest day non-negotiable
3. Do not stack two limit sessions if fingers are still cooked
4. Deload every 4-6 weeks if fatigue accumulates

## How to Adjust by Experience Level

### Beginner

Prioritize movement quality. More easy mileage, less max effort.

### Intermediate

Keep two quality sessions and one project day. Track fatigue honestly.

### Advanced

Use tighter session objectives and higher discipline on recovery.

## Signs Your Week Is Mis-Structured

- frequent elbow/finger tweaks
- constant fatigue before warm-up finishes
- no send improvements for multiple weeks
- motivation drops despite high effort

When these show up, reduce intensity first, not just volume.

## Progress Metrics to Track Weekly

- number of quality hard attempts
- best send grade this week
- recovery score (simple 1-5 self-rating)
- finger readiness before session

## Final Take

A good week is not about training more.

It is about placing the right stress on the right day, then recovering enough to absorb it.

If your structure improves, your climbing usually follows.

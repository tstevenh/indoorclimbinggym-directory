## Choose Mini If...

Mini looks like the better fit if you want:

- guided app testing and training
- lightweight and travel-friendly
- the most concrete public spec list currently available
- a product page that makes the offer easier to understand at a glance

On Climbro's Mini page, the public details currently include:

- compatibility with Android 5.0+ or iOS 12.0+
- Bluetooth 5.0 Low Energy
- 100Hz high sampling rate
- 6 hold sizes from 1.3 cm to 4 cm
- lightweight and portable

The page also uses buyer-friendly language like:

- "Carry Climbro Mini wherever you need it"
- use cases include warm-up, rehab, testing, and training
- movement options include pulling from below and pulling from the side

That combination makes Mini the easiest current entry point to understand from the public site alone.

## Consider Pro If...

Pro is harder to recommend as the default, but it may still be the right fit if:

- you are already convinced by Climbro's overall training-system pitch
- the broader flagship positioning is what appeals to you
- you are comfortable moving forward without the same level of public spec detail the Mini page provides

On the public Pro page, Climbro's top-level positioning is broader. The page describes Climbro as:

- "the most advanced tool for training and measuring climbing specific strength to date"
- a platform for "next level personalized training"

The Pro page also routes into Climbro's broader shop and FAQ flow, where Climbro publishes:

- buy new
- buy refurbished
- rent
- 30-day home trial
- warranty information
- app subscription information

That is useful, but it is also the main limitation for this comparison:

- the Pro page does **not** currently publish the same kind of detailed public spec list that the Mini page publishes

So `Pro` is more of a considered path than a default recommendation.

It makes more sense for buyers who are already mostly sold on Climbro and are comfortable moving with less front-end detail.

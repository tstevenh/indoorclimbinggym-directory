## Quick Answer

What Climbro's official site makes explicit today:

- the **Mini** page publishes a clear public feature list and spec list
- the **Pro** page is more general and emphasizes Climbro as a tool for training and measuring climbing-specific strength
- Climbro currently markets both product exploration and lower-risk entry paths through `Rental` and the `30-Day Home Trial`

If you want the most clearly documented option on the public site today, the Mini page currently provides more concrete details.

## What the Official Mini Page Says

On Climbro's Mini page, the current public feature bullets include:

- guided app testing and training
- lightweight and travel-friendly
- 30-day home trial

The Mini overview also lists:

- compatibility with Android 5.0+ or iOS 12.0+
- Bluetooth 5.0 Low Energy
- 100Hz high sampling rate
- 6 hold sizes from 1.3 cm to 4 cm
- lightweight and portable

The Mini page also says:

- "Carry Climbro Mini wherever you need it"
- use cases include warm-up, rehab, testing, and training
- movement options include pulling from below and pulling from the side

## What the Official Pro Page Says

On the public Pro page, Climbro's current top-level positioning is broader. The page describes Climbro as:

- "the most advanced tool for training and measuring climbing specific strength to date"
- a platform for "next level personalized training"

The Pro page also routes into Climbro's broader shop and FAQ system, where Climbro publishes:

- buy new
- buy refurbished
- rent
- 30-day home trial
- warranty information
- app subscription information

The public Pro page does **not** currently publish the same kind of detailed public spec list that the Mini page publishes.
